--- BioRED RELATION SELECTION GUIDE ---

Use 'Association' ONLY when:
  - The relationship between two entities is mentioned but the direction of effect is completely neutral, unclear, or not specified.
  - A whole Gene/GeneProduct is associated with or linked to a disease without a specific active causal mechanism (e.g., "SLC30A8, HHEX, KCNQ1 confer a risk of T2DM" -> Association).
  - General non-specific associations where no causal or treatment direction is stated or implied.

Use 'Positive_Correlation' when:
  - One entity CLEARLY increases, upregulates, activates, promotes, or enhances the other.
  - A chemical or drug causes, induces, triggers, or increases the risk/severity of a disease, symptom, complication, or adverse effect (e.g., "cocaine-induced stroke", "lithium causes polyuria", "alcohol increases risk of breast cancer", "drug causes arrhythmia").
  - A specific genetic variant, mutation, allele, codon, or polymorphism increases susceptibility to or risk of a disease (e.g., "rs13266634 polymorphism increases susceptibility to T2DM" -> Positive_Correlation).
  - A Gene/GeneProduct has an active, causal pathway described where its presence/activation directly promotes the disease or its absence/ablation prevents the disease (e.g., "Absence of PKCa prevents NDI development" -> PKCa has Positive_Correlation with NDI).
  - A gene/protein activates or upregulates a downstream target.

Use 'Negative_Correlation' when:
  - One entity CLEARLY decreases, inhibits, suppresses, downregulates, or protects against the other.
  - A chemical or drug treats, prevents, cures, reduces, or protects against a disease, symptom, complication, or adverse effect (e.g., "metformin treats diabetes", "aspirin reduces risk of heart attack", "lifestyle changes prevent prediabetes").
  - A genetic variant, mutation, or polymorphism decreases susceptibility to or protects against a disease.
  - A drug/chemical reduces protein expression or lowers biomarker levels.

Use 'Drug_Interaction' ONLY when:
  - Two DRUGS or CHEMICALS interact with each other pharmacologically.
  - Both Subject AND Object are drugs/chemicals, not a drug and a biological entity/symptom.

Use 'Bind' ONLY when:
  - There is direct physical binding, receptor-ligand interaction, or molecular complex formation.
