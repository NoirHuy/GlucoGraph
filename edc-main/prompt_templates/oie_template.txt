PART 3: SEVEN UNBREAKABLE EXTRACTION RULES
════════════════════════════════════════════════

RULE 1 — DIRECTIONALITY IS MANDATORY (Fix Logic Inversion):
  The direction of every relation is fixed. Verify subject and object before writing ANY triple.

  1a. 'may be treated by' is ALWAYS: [Disease/Condition] → may be treated by → [Treatment/Medication].
      A [Treatment] can NEVER be the SUBJECT of 'may be treated by'.
      ✗ WRONG: ['basal insulin', 'may be treated by', 'diabetes']
      ✗ WRONG: ['prandial insulin', 'may be treated by', 'diabetes']
      ✓ CORRECT: ['diabetes', 'may be treated by', 'basal insulin']

  1b. 'manifestation of' is ALWAYS: [Symptom/Complication] → manifestation of → [Primary Disease].
      Prediabetes PRECEDES Type 2 Diabetes — use 'may be associated disease of disease' instead.
      ✗ WRONG: ['prediabetes', 'manifestation of', 'Type 2 Diabetes']
      ✓ CORRECT: ['diabetic neuropathy', 'manifestation of', 'diabetes mellitus']

  1c. 'associated finding of' is ALWAYS: [Symptom/Clinical Finding] → associated finding of → [Disease].
      ✗ WRONG: ['weight loss', 'associated finding of', 'dyslipidemia']
      ✓ CORRECT: ['dyslipidemia', 'may be treated by', 'weight loss']

  1d. DRUG/TREATMENT DIRECTION IS ALWAYS: [Disease] → may be treated by → [Drug/Treatment]. NEVER REVERSE.
      ✗ WRONG: ['metformin', 'may be treated by', 'Type 2 Diabetes']
      ✓ CORRECT: ['Type 2 Diabetes', 'may be treated by', 'metformin']

  1e. 'is preferred over' is ALWAYS: [Preferred Drug] → is preferred over → [Less-Preferred Drug].
      ✗ WRONG: ['NPH', 'is preferred over', 'analog insulins']
      ✓ CORRECT: ['analog insulins', 'is preferred over', 'NPH']

  1f. 'has adverse effect' is ALWAYS: [Drug/Treatment] → has adverse effect → [Symptom/Condition].
      ✗ WRONG: ['hypoglycemia', 'has adverse effect', 'premixed insulin']
      ✓ CORRECT: ['premixed insulin', 'has adverse effect', 'hypoglycemia']

RULE 2 — ONE ENTITY PER SLOT, NO COMPOUND STRINGS (Fix Atomicity Bug):
  NEVER group multiple diseases, symptoms, or treatments into a single string using commas or 'and'.
  If the text mentions multiple items, CREATE A SEPARATE TRIPLE FOR EACH ITEM.
  ✗ WRONG: ['Type 2 Diabetes', 'associated condition of', 'heart failure, chronic kidney disease']
  ✓ CORRECT: ['Type 2 Diabetes', 'associated condition of', 'heart failure']
             ['Type 2 Diabetes', 'associated condition of', 'chronic kidney disease']

RULE 3 — STRICT ENTITY CLASS ENFORCEMENT (Fix Domain/Range Violations):
  Words containing 'therapy', 'modification', 'intervention', 'medication', 'treatment', or 'pharmacotherapy'

  Treatments CANNOT be the subject of 'associated condition of', 'co-occurs with', or 'may be associated disease of disease'.

RULE 4 — NO CONCEPTUAL DOCUMENTS AS TREATMENTS (Fix Guidelines-as-Therapy Bug):
  Words like 'Model', 'Guideline', 'Recommendations', 'Algorithm', 'Standards', 'Framework', 'Plan', or 'Roadmap'
  refer to THEORETICAL DOCUMENTS, NOT physical interventions.
  FORBIDDEN: These document names as the OBJECT of 'may be treated by'.
  → INSTEAD: Extract the ACTUAL intervention the sentence recommends.

RULE 5 — ABSTRACT LOGIC INTEGRITY CHECK (Fix Abstract Inversion):
  Medical goals, outcomes, and metrics such as 'glycemic control', 'glycemic targets', 'metabolic control',
  'blood pressure control', or 'weight management' are OUTCOMES/GOALS, NOT diseases being treated.
  They CANNOT be the Subject of 'may be treated by'. They CANNOT be the Object of 'may be treated by'.
  ✗ WRONG: ['glycemic control', 'may be treated by', 'insulin']
  ✗ WRONG: ['basal insulin', 'may be treated by', 'glycemic targets']
  ✓ CORRECT: ['Type 2 Diabetes', 'has evaluation', 'glycemic control']

RULE 6 — COMPARISON/PREFERENCE PATTERN (NEW):
  When text says drug A "is preferred over" drug B, or A is "recommended over" B:
  - Use the relation 'is preferred over': [Preferred Drug] → is preferred over → [Less-Preferred Drug]
  - Also extract: [Disease] → may be treated by → [Preferred Drug]
  - Do NOT mark the less-preferred drug as "contraindicated" — being less preferred is NOT the same as being contraindicated.
  ✗ WRONG: ['diabetes', 'has contraindicated drug', 'NPH']  (NPH is valid, just less preferred)
  ✓ CORRECT: ['analog insulins', 'is preferred over', 'NPH']
             ['diabetes', 'may be treated by', 'analog insulins']

RULE 7 — CONDITIONAL/TITRATION/DOSE PATTERN (NEW):
  When text describes dose adjustments with conditions (if glucose > X, then adjust dose by Y%):
  - Use 'has dose adjustment': [Drug] → has dose adjustment → [Dosage Value]
  - Use 'has clinical threshold': [Clinical Metric] → has clinical threshold → [Dosage Value]
  - The condition (e.g., "fasting blood glucose >180 mg/dL") and the action (e.g., "increase by 20%") are SEPARATE triples.
  - When text says to DISCONTINUE drug A after starting drug B:
    Use 'should be discontinued with': [Drug A] → should be discontinued with → [Drug B]

  Example text: "The basal insulin dose can be increased by 20% if the fasting blood glucose is >180 mg/dL."
  ✓ CORRECT: ['diabetes', 'may be treated by', 'basal insulin']
